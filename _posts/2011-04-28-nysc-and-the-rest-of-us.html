--- published: true title: NYSC and the rest of us layout: post --- The wrong things we place values on in this country has not failed to
amaze me. Really, I wonder why whoever came up with NYSC thought I owe
Nigeria a one year compulsory service. Well, compulsory not like do or
die; but compulsory like there are some appointments and higher
education you can't take. You get.

Ok fine, the government gave me free education and provided for all my
study course and in exchange require a compulsory one year service,
shouldn't I be treated fair at all?

By the way, this is not even an argument of if NYSC should be scrapped
on not. If the government thinks I owe her a one year compulsory
service year, shouldn't my welfare be top priority? Oh, you havent
seen my bathing water at camp right? The government should understand
that not all of us finish school, have nothing to do and would rather
patiently wait for service to be able to earn a meagre N10,000/month.
(Yeah, I heard N18,000+ has been approved, but I'm yet to see that
effect in my account). I know the service is a good exchange program
and all, but my number one argument is it should be optional. I mean
truly optional - the one with no 'side effects'.

Two, corps security and welfare should be top priority. Here is one
month into service and a lot of my friends are still prowling the
streets of Cross River looking for places of primary assignment. And
the one I got? Nice one, good place. Only that no accommodation
provided (in a City I don't know anyone in) and I won't be paid for
the first 3-4 months. I can only always be grateful to religious
bodies like NCCF, RCCF and co for providing accomodation and feeding
for corp members for as long as they want to stay. (There are few
catch and rules attached though). And security? That is worth a whole
blog rant on its own. I still don't understand why the government
still post corpers to crisis prone areas like Jos, Kaduna, Kano and co
every year even in the midst of such crisis despite the many lives
lost year in, year out. I must say I really commend the Lagos, Ondo
and Ogun government for withdrawing their serving citizens from this
areas but question now is what is the FG's plan on security for the
next batch?
